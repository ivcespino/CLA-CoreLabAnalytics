Official Research Title

The Relationship Between Computer Laboratory Usage and Preliminary and Midterm Performance in Selected Core BSIT Subjects at STI College Malolos with the Integration of a Web-Based Research and Visualization Tool

Background of the Study

At STI College Malolos, computer laboratories are central to IT training, serving as the environment where lecture material translates into applied skills. Local work shows that strong ICT integration is tied to better student outcomes (Escubido et al., 2025), while international studies point to the way access to electronic facilities strengthens student competence (Sharif-Nia et al., 2024).

However, most research focuses on end-of-term results. What remains less examined is the longitudinal shift— specifically, how changes in laboratory usage frequency and intensity correspond to measurable performance change between the Preliminary and Midterm grading periods.

Statement of the Problem

Central Question:

Does computer laboratory usage— measured through attended laboratory hours per week and the percentage of hands-on activity— correspond to changes in academic performance between the Preliminary and Midterm grading periods?

Research Objectives

  • Measure Usage: Determine the frequency (hours/week) and intensity (hands-on %) of lab usage.
  • Analyze Correlation: Determine relationships between usage and Preliminary/Midterm grades.
  • Assess Impact: Assess if increased usage corresponds to a positive Performance Change Score.
  • Develop Tool: Create a web-based platform for visualization and dissemination.

Hypotheses

Alternative (H₁)

Higher laboratory usage is associated with a positive change in academic performance between grading periods.

Null (H₀₁)

Laboratory usage is not associated with a significant change in academic performance.

Significance of the Study

This study delivers empirical evidence to support pedagogical decisions regarding the optimal use of ICT resources.

For Students

Encourages intentional use of hands-on practice for skill development.

For Institutions

Guides decisions on capacity, maintenance, and allocation of equipment.

Research Methodology

analytics

Research Design

Quantitative Correlational Research Design. This method assesses the linear association between continuous numerical data (lab hours, hands-on time, grades) without manipulating variables.

groups

Participants & Sampling

Purposive Sampling. The study targets 145 respondents from a population of 501 BSIT students. Participants are selected based on the non-negotiable criteria of possessing paired academic records (Prelim & Midterm).

model_training

Data Analysis Plan

The study utilizes Pearson r for correlation and Multiple Linear Regression to test predictive power. The "Performance Change Score" (Midterm - Prelim) serves as the primary dependent variable.
